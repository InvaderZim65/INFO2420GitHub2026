Web Site Critiques

Website: Jami Lin - http://www.jamilin.com/

1. What was your first impression of this site?:

My first impression was that this website is extremely bright and very busy. The bright green background and the rainbow colors caught my eye immediately, but it felt a little cluttered with too many different font sizes.

2. How does this site organize the flow of information?:

The site organizes information from the top down. It has a big banner at the top, followed by a navigation bar with links, and then columns of pictures and text below that explain the different services.

3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?:

The structure is a little confusing to me. There are a lot of different topics mixed together, like skin care and "Galactic Spirit," which makes it hard to know exactly what the main point of the business is right away.

4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?:

I think the graphics distract from the site. The header image has text written over a photo which makes it look messy, and the cutout photos of the owner look a little bit amateur compared to modern professional sites.

5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?:

The home page loaded very fast. Since it is mostly simple images and text without heavy animation, the load time was very reasonable.

6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?:

The text was somewhat hard to read. The white text on the bright green background is straining on the eyes, and the text inside the navigation bar buttons is a bit small.

7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?:

Yes, the site has a navigation bar across the middle of the page. It is very colorful (rainbow style), which makes it easy to see, but the colors are very intense.

8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?:

The site definitely has a repeated look and feel. It uses a lot of neon greens and a rainbow color palette that matches the "Chakras" and "ColorAlchemy" themes mentioned on the home page.

9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?:

The site does not seem to respond well to smaller windows. When I made the window smaller, the images didn't stack or move; they just stayed in the same place, which means you have to scroll sideways.

10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?:

The most unusual thing about this site is the combination of topics. It is very unique to see a site that offers "Organic Skin Care" and "Quantum Mayan Insights" in the same place.
